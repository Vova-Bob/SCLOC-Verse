# Aegis Interface — Design Philosophy for SCLOC-Verse Hangar Timer Icon

## 1. Visual Language
The icon speaks the dialect of military-grade aerospace HUD interfaces: clean, cold, and authoritative. Every line suggests machined titanium or projected laser wireframe. The composition is anchored by a top-down ship silhouette nested inside a segmented cycle ring, framed by the ghost of a hangar bay. Nothing is decorative; every element carries meaning.

## 2. Form and Geometry
Forms are reduced to their essential silhouettes: a delta-wing hull, a thin circular timer ring broken into discreet segments, and a wide trapezoidal hangar mouth drawn with razor-thin strokes. The ship hovers at the center, ringed by time. The hangar contour recedes behind, giving depth without complexity. All geometry is vector-precise, pixel-snapped, and balanced around a single visual center.

## 3. Color and Light
The palette is limited to three tones: electric cyan (#57C7FF) for primary structure and active edges, pale ice-blue (#AEEBFF) for secondary glow and highlights, and near-black slate (#0E1620 / #1A2430) for recessed shadows. Gradients are forbidden. Light is implied through hard edges and a subtle outer glow, never through blended shading.

## 4. Scale and Craft
The icon must read instantly at 64×64 and remain crisp at 256×256. Line weights are scaled with the resolution: hairline strokes at small sizes, slightly heavier at large sizes. Glow intensity is tuned per scale so the 64×64 version does not blur and the 256×256 version does not look washed out. The result feels like an official ship-stamp from the Star Citizen universe.

## 5. Restraint
No text. No numerals. No LED dots. No clock hands. The icon communicates "hangar" through architecture, "timer" through the segmented ring, and "Star Citizen" through the ship silhouette and HUD aesthetic. It is a symbol, not an illustration.
